## Overview

ReplyIQ's Emoji Suggestions feature helps you boost engagement and add personality to your social media replies. Using advanced AI, ReplyIQ analyzes the context of each post and reply to recommend relevant, platform-appropriate emojis—making your responses more expressive and engaging.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Context Analysis">
    The AI analyzes the original post and your reply draft for sentiment, topic, and platform conventions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Emoji Selection">
    Based on the analysis, ReplyIQ recommends a set of relevant emojis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="User Interaction">
    You can preview, select, and insert suggested emojis with a single click or keyboard shortcut.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customization">
    Adjust emoji usage in your extension or dashboard settings (e.g., max emojis per reply).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Usage Tips & Best Practices

<Tip>
  **Keep it relevant:** Only use emojis that enhance your message and fit the context.
</Tip>
<Tip>
  **Don't overdo it:** 1-3 emojis per reply is usually best for most platforms.
</Tip>
<Tip>
  **Match the platform:** Use more playful emojis on Twitter/Instagram, and more reserved ones on LinkedIn.
</Tip>
<Tip>
  **Preview before posting:** Make sure the emojis support your intended tone and meaning.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No Emoji Suggestions Appearing">
    - Make sure emoji suggestions are enabled in your extension or dashboard settings
    - Check that you're signed in and connected to ReplyIQ
    - Try refreshing the social media page
    - If using the API, ensure `includeEmojis` is set to `true` in your request
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Irrelevant or Repetitive Emojis">
    - Provide more context in your reply or original post
    - Adjust your tone or platform settings
    - Manually remove or change suggested emojis as needed
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Platform-Specific Issues">
    - Some platforms may limit emoji rendering or support
    - Check for extension updates if issues persist
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
